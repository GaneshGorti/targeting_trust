Example: How Your Income Is Calculated

In your group, 2 citizens earn {{ piece_rate }} ECU per slider and 2 citizens earn {{ other_rate }} ECU per slider. You are one of the citizens earning {{ piece_rate }} ECU per slider. All citizens pay a tax of 3 ECU per reported slider.

Your example ({{ piece_rate }} ECU per slider):

Suppose you complete {{ example_sliders }} sliders correctly.

Your gross income:
{{ example_sliders }} × {{ piece_rate }} ECU = {{ gross }} ECU

Your tax:
{{ example_sliders }} × 3 ECU = {{ tax }} ECU

Your net income:
{{ gross }} − {{ tax }} = {{ net }} ECU

Example for the 2 other citizens ({{ other_rate }} ECU per slider):

Suppose they complete {{ example_sliders }} sliders correctly.

Their gross income:
{{ example_sliders }} × {{ other_rate }} ECU = {{ other_gross }} ECU

Their tax:
{{ example_sliders }} × 3 ECU = {{ tax }} ECU

Their net income:
{{ other_gross }} − {{ tax }} = {{ other_net }} ECU

Remember, the Administrator was asked to count the correctly placed sliders accurately and received 1 ECU for every slider they count. They were not allowed to misreport the number of completed sliders. Your tax is calculated based on this reported slider count.
